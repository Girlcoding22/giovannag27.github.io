What is E.Coli?

E.Coli was discovered in the year 1885 by Theodor Escherich. E.Coli is usually passed on by contaminated water or food. It is known as the most studied bacteria. It is important to know that only a certain type of E.Coli is actually harmful to us. One example of bad E.Coli is the 0157:H7 this is more related to food poisoning, not many people knew about it until the year 1987. This specific part of E.Coli (0157:H7) has made a recent comeback here in California.

What does this map read?

The map on the left shows the number of people that were infected with E.Coli during the recent outbreak with romaine lettuce throughout the United States (2018). What does E.Coli cause?

The most recent outbreak was in 2018 and California had over 40 people that were ill. E.Coli is manifested in many ways, some common symptoms include: abdominal cramping, gas, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, fatigue, fever or sudden diarrhea. Many people recommend at home care which consist of drinking plenty of water, getting a well rest and being aware of your well-being. Elderly and young people are more susceptible to this disease due to their developing or weakening immune system, they usually experience kidney failure as well as bloody diarrhea.According to the CDC 49 out of 210 people who got ill were from California this is 23.3% of the population that got ill, which is more than Pennsylvania which only had 11.4% of the population.
